Reading

Posted on 2020-07-10 Edited on 2020-07-12 In 日志
Symbols count in article: 3.3k Reading time ≈ 3 mins.

Experience

Reading was never my thing untill I came to America. Back in China, I have only read textbooks, never completed a single book outside classroom. I don’t like it because I consider it very inefficient compared to videos which is attracting and visually understandable text are hard to understand and I don’t have the patience to read along 300 pages.

After I came to America, I struggle in reading, not only because I have never read, but also because it’s in English. I usually need to spend 3 minutes, back and forth between translations and boom,to finish a page without full understanding. But I can only read it since it’s class’s requirement.

What changes my attitude towards reading is, maybe surprsing to you, SAT. As we all know, SAT has a difficult reading part, which is even more complicated for me, a never read Chinese. When I took classes, I found it hard to follow because I hardly understand what the passage is talking about, especially these historical passages. I spent lots of time practicing but with low payback, I still suck at reading. I was frustrated. Once my friend told me the best way to improve was to read more english books, no matter the kind, the time, and the author.

Comparing to do meaningless practices, I decided to read. The first book I read in my own will is 1984.

I spennt nearly two months to finish this, and I can only get the main idea of each chapter without remembering more details in major conflicts. Since the book was similar to Brave New World I read in class, I have less trouble in understanding the world view in 1948 and the main idea. The book talks about a world wihch is controlled by three powers and they all have the same way of ruling people. “who controls the past controls the future” is the slogans in the main hero’s country. The governing party controls people in every aspect, words, language, sex, past, freedom and they have a telescreen everywhere to observe people’s move to connfirm whether they are making Thoughtcrime.

I think this Utopian world is one of the finest and most refined one I have seen(I have only seen three, hunger game, brave new world and 1948). Totalitarianism is depicted very vivid, the extreme conntrol over the citizens secure the absolute loyalty. Even someone who feels something is wrong about this word, the government has a place to brainwash this idea and replace it with wonderful things about this world. What impressed me most is a sentence uttered by Winston,”Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two makes four”. This sentence expressed the power party exert on the information, as well the opposing emotion Winston potentially has.

I don’t want to say too much about the plot. It’s a story which you can feel the helplessness and understand it, and I think it gives a picture of what future maybe like.

Other books

After reading 1984, I found out that I read faster than before and understand more quickly. So I started to read other books, I once watched the series of And Then There Is None, I was fasnicated by the plot designed and the character shaping. It’s written by Agatha Christie, a famous writer well known for its detective stories. I like her style of writing, it creates a bigger picture and shows each character’s deep aspect in the process.

The Murder of Roger Ackroyd

The ABC Murder

The Mysterious Affair at Styles

The essence of dective story is to deceive the reader, and Agatha did a great job in that. Everyone involved in the conflict is realted to the murder, the murder was a combination of series of mistakes and malice, and you can only see the truth when Hercule Poirot reveal the whole picture in front of you.

I hope you shares some of the feelings I have for reading. Reading is not as boring as I thought, and it gives a more vivid description of the story than depicted in the tv series or videos. When I read these lines, I can also experience the beauty possessed in English, the language is wonderful.
